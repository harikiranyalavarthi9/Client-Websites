    Welcome to River Edge Mansion!


    A Bed and Breakfast on the Erie Canal in the Syracuse Region of Central New York.







    You will fall in love with this restored Greek revival mansion on the banks of the Oneida River just 12 miles from Syracuse in Central New York. The 1818 Schroeppel Homestead, now known as River Edge Mansion, was home to the first school room in the area and is listed in the National Register of Historic Places. The Mansion's fascinating history includes the legend of a French princess and its builder George Casper von Schroeppel, an instrumental founder of the Erie Canal.

    This lovely old home has been restored to display its original charm and elegance and many of the original features still exist. The Mansion retains its beautiful hardwood floors, wood spindle staircases, interior window shutters and a cherry ceiling in the dining room. This nearly 200 year old structure has five marble and stone fireplaces. Pocket doors and a tin ceiling remain as do exquisite hand-carved moldings and cornices. Four beautifully appointed guestrooms furnished with fine antiques and oriental rugs will warm the hearts of the most discerning guest. This gracious bed & breakfast allows you to escape from the ordinary.

    Tastefully decorated rooms create an atmosphere of elegance and refinement yet allow you to completely relax and feel right at home. Languish in a whirlpool bath, read by a marble fireplace or dream under a canopy bed. Spacious surroundings inside and out, give you an opportunity for complete privacy or gracious sharing. A private pathway leads you directly to the Oneida River where you will find private docking facilities with water and electricity hookup for boating, fishing and swimming. Dock your boat and join us for breakfast and dinner!

    Captain Steve will happily take you for a riverboat cruise aboard our canal boat, the Ella C. or join us for a two and a half hour dinner cruise through Lock 23 or to the river village of Phoenix while enjoying a delicious dinner grilled on the boat.

    Combine all of this with fabulous gourmet breakfasts and you may never want to leave!

Our hostess was kind enough to make dinner reservations for us at a fabulous restaurant in Baldwinsville that sent a LIMO to the inn to pick us up and return us from dinner. I was in complete awe over that!!! The gourmet breakfast was the perfect ending to a perfect overnight experience. If you're looking for a B&B in the Oswego area, this is a choice I highly recommend.
